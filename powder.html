To determine thermal conductivity of insulating powder.

In many heat transfer equipments, heat loss to surrounding S to be tiaintained economy. In such cases they are lagged by materials of lower thermal conductivity, which are referred as insulators. Powders have the advantage that they ean take any shape between any two confirming surfaces. In addition its conductivity will be much lower than that of the solid from which the powder has been made. This is because of very large number of air space in between parices, which have much lower thermal conductivity values. Thermal conductivity ot sucn material is a complicated function of the geometry of the particles, particle thermal conductivity, the nature of heat transfer, conduction, convection and radiation in air spaces, which is determined by the air space size and temperature level etc.
